The Wave-Particle Duality

In the quantum realm, entities exhibit properties that challenge our classical intuition. A photon or an electron is neither strictly a wave nor strictly a particle; instead, it is a quantum object that can manifest as either depending on the observation setup.

Equation 1.1: The de Broglie Relation

λ = h / p

Where λ is wavelength, h is Planck's constant, and p is momentum.

The Schrödinger Equation

To describe how the quantum state of a physical system changes with time, we utilize the Schrödinger equation. It is the cornerstone of non-relativistic quantum mechanics.

  • Wave Function (Ψ): A mathematical description of the quantum state of a system.
  • Probability Density: The square of the absolute value of the wave function represents the likelihood of finding a particle at a specific point.
  • Superposition: A system exists in all theoretically possible states simultaneously until it is measured.

Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

One of the most profound implications of quantum mechanics is the fundamental limit to the precision with which certain pairs of physical properties can be known simultaneously.
